# coJournalist overview

> coJournalist is AI-powered monitoring infrastructure for journalists. It runs scouts on schedules, watches public sources, extracts atomic facts, deduplicates across sources, and hands the results to journalists and AI assistants as a searchable knowledge base with an explicit verification step.

coJournalist is built for local and investigative reporting. The public product story is:

- Watch pages, social profiles, local news, and council sites without living in tabs and feeds.
- Turn recurring monitoring into structured information units instead of chat transcripts.
- Let agents draft, triage, and search while journalists remain the verifier.
- Work through the web app, MCP, REST API, and the `cojo` CLI.
- Stay portable: hosted at cojournalist.ai, source-available, and self-hostable.

## Core benefits

- **Schedule from a chat**: tell your agent what to monitor and let it create or manage scouts.
- **Every story, once**: dedup collapses the same development across outlets and follow-up coverage.
- **A newsroom database, not a chat log**: information units are citeable records with dates, sources, and verification state.
- **Fits existing workflows**: output is useful for briefs, scripts, CMS exports, and editorial review.
- **Human verification stays in the loop**: unverified units are leads, not publishable facts.

## What it monitors

- **Page Scout**: track one URL for meaningful changes.
- **Beat Scout**: monitor a beat by topic or geography, with source-mode control.
- **Social Scout**: track social accounts, including deletions.
- **Civic Scout**: monitor councils, agendas, minutes, and PDFs for promises and decisions.
